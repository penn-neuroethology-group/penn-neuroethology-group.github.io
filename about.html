Penn Neuroethology Group

The Penn Neuroethology Group is a leading research collective at the University of Pennsylvania, dedicated to advancing our understanding of the neural mechanisms underlying natural behavior in animals. Our interdisciplinary approach integrates computational models, deep learning techniques, and innovative neurotechnology to explore the complexities of the brain.

Mission

Our mission is to decode the neural language of social interaction and cognitive processes in animals. By merging empirical research with computational methods, we aim to construct detailed models that can predict and elucidate behavior. Through our work, we strive to not only advance scientific knowledge but also to apply our findings to real-world challenges in medicine, artificial intelligence, and conservation.
