Introduction

The Penn Neuroethology Group is a leading research collective at the University of Pennsylvania, dedicated to advancing our understanding of the neural mechanisms underlying social behavior in primates. Our interdisciplinary approach integrates computational models, deep learning techniques, and innovative neurotechnology to explore the complexities of the primate brain.

Founded in [Year] by a team of pioneering researchers, the group has grown to include faculty and students from a variety of disciplines. Our Mission

Our mission is to decode the neural language of social interaction and cognitive processes in primates. By merging empirical research with computational methods, we aim to construct detailed models that can predict and elucidate behavior. Through our work, we strive to not only advance scientific knowledge but also to apply our findings to real-world challenges in medicine, artificial intelligence, and conservation.

Methodologies

Employing a multidisciplinary toolkit, we engage in deep phenotyping of social interactions in naturalistic settings. Our labs are equipped with state-of-the-art neuroimaging technology, wireless neurorecording devices, and custom-developed software for pose estimation and behavior analysis. Our computational models are honed through machine learning algorithms and big data analytics, providing a window into the complex dance of neuronal networks.
